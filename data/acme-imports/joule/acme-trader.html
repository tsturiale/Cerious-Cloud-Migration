ES / NQ

Momentum-sensitive Spread = ES - 0.2667 * NQ

Buy spread: long ES / short NQ. This is a fade of Nasdaq outperformance or a broadening trade.

Sell spread: short ES / long NQ. This is a mega-cap tech leadership trade.

Nuance: ES/NQ mean reversion is less reliable when AI, semiconductors, or mega-cap quality are leading with genuine earnings momentum.

YM / ES

Calmer leadership spread Spread = YM - 6.6667 * ES

Buy spread: long YM / short ES. You want Dow value, industrials, financials, or defensive cyclicals to outperform.

Sell spread: short YM / long ES. You want S&P mega-cap and growth weight to outperform the Dow.

Nuance: YM is price-weighted and only 30 stocks, so single-stock Dow composition matters more than in ES.

RTY / ES

Credit and rates sensitive Spread = RTY - 0.4265 * ES

Buy spread: long RTY / short ES. You want small caps, domestic cyclicals, regional banks, and breadth to improve.

Sell spread: short RTY / long ES. You want large-cap quality, mega-cap tech, or balance-sheet strength to dominate.

Nuance: RTY can remain cheap for structural reasons when rates are high or credit spreads widen.

ATR and Z-Score Engine

ItemDefinitionUse
Spread Series Prefer synchronized intraday spread bars rolled into daily OHLC; fall back to matched daily OHLC bars for the displayed synthetic formula. Creates spread-native ATR and raw study values.
ATR Average the last 3-session ATR and 30-session ATR. The theoretical market applies half of this ATR to each side of VWAP. Volatility unit for the raw technical read.
Session VWAP Use synchronized intraday prices and volume when available; fall back to time-weighted average when volume is missing. Raw midpoint reference for discretionary interpretation.
Z-Score (Spread - Mean) / ATR plus an intraday VWAP z-score using ATR/2. Cross-spread ranking and intraday trigger pressure.
ATR Percentile Current ATR vs one-year ATR distribution. Size down when volatility is unusually elevated.

Execution Rules

Base entry: Enter at +/-1.5 ATR only when the macro regime, GOOSE direction, and live spread signal agree. If only two of three agree, cut the first clip in half.
Stress rule: If ATR percentile is above 80, cut size by 50%, widen bands to +/-2 ATR, and require the live z-score to stop worsening before entry.
No-trade rule: Do not buy RTY/ES just because it is statistically cheap if credit spreads are widening, rates are rising, and regional banks are weak.
Timing rule: Avoid initiating new layered positions in the first 3-5 minutes after major data releases. Let the synthetic spread print a first reaction, a pullback, and then a second attempt before committing.
Confirmation rule: For mean reversion, require price to re-enter from beyond the band. Example: buy only after the spread trades below -1.5 ATR and then recovers back above -1.4 ATR.
Layering rule: Use three clips: 40% at trigger, 30% at an additional 0.35 ATR extension, 30% only after stabilization. Never add simply because the trade is losing.
Profit rule: Take 1/3 off at the rolling mean, another 1/3 at +0.5 ATR in your favor, and trail the final 1/3 with a 0.5 ATR giveback or a GOOSE regime flip.
Invalidation rule: Exit if live z-score extends 0.75 ATR beyond your final layer without reversal, or if macro score flips against the trade by two factors.

For intraday execution, maintain time-of-day ATR bands. Cash open, Fed/CPI/jobs windows, and closing imbalance windows should not use the same thresholds as midday liquidity.

Order Layering Techniques

TechniqueHow To Use ItBest Context
Passive first clipJoin or shade the synthetic bid/offer for 40% of planned size at the first qualified band touch.Normal liquidity, no urgent catalyst.
Extension layerPlace the second 30% only if the spread extends another 0.35 ATR and the macro read has not deteriorated.Mean-reversion setup with controlled volatility.
Stabilization layerFinal 30% enters after the spread stops making new extremes and reclaims a small threshold, such as 0.10-0.20 ATR.When timing precision matters more than catching the exact low/high.
Scratch layerIf first clip fills and immediately loses 0.35 ATR without stabilization, scratch instead of adding.Fast markets, news shocks, weak confirmation.
Mean exit ladderScale out at mean, +0.5 ATR, then trail the remainder.Any spread mean-reversion trade.

Source Notes

Daily ATR source: ATR is calculated by ACME Trader from the server chart-history cache.

Live cockpit source: the Linux Databento Python API price server owns outright last price and top-of-book. The browser receives normalized server-sent events and repaints terminal windows from those events.

Live platform scaffold: /api/realtime/stream exposes sequenced SSE envelopes for platform, market-depth, news, analysis, data-status, orders, fills, algos, and heartbeat. The DOM windows merge those streams only at the view layer.

GOOSE source: combines refreshed futures history, macro regime inputs, spread z-scores, volume context, delayed live quote layer, public ETF factor proxies, CFTC Commitments of Traders positioning, and available public market headline feeds including Yahoo Finance, Bloomberg Markets, WSJ Markets, CNBC Markets, and NBC Business when their RSS endpoints respond. GOOSE is a decision-support rule engine, not an execution system.

Contract spec anchors: CME contract pages for ES, NQ, RTY, and YM.

Macro regime source: the local server fetches latest free daily Yahoo chart history for ^VIX, ^TNX, HYG, KRE, RSP, SPY, ES=F, and RTY=F, then scores volatility, rates, credit, regional banks, breadth, and small-cap leadership.

This is a risk-management framework, not financial advice. Use exchange rules, broker margin, live market data, and your own risk limits before trading.
